A Midnight Picnic
2021
Medium: Pen and ink on paper
Process and intention: This piece is my take on surrealism. My unfamiliarity with this artist movement made the process for Midnight Picnic to be challenging yet somehow liberating. Taking inspiration from one of my favourite artists, Max Ernest and Tim Burtonâ€™s reiteration of Alice In Wonderland, themes of nature and dark fantasy were mindfully incorporated throughout my piece. With full creative liberty, I personified plants and objects but mainly ants in my piece. I worked fully in black ink, intending to make use of negative space rather than positive space to make a bolder statement. Additionally, I liked how illustrative the piece looked without the use of colour. I further took inspiration from artist Bridget Riley and specifically the black & white Op Art she made during the 60s. I believe that incorporating the black and white checkered pattern throughout my pieces was a thoughtful choice, as not only does it bring interest by introducing movement, but is also fitting as a reference to red and white gingham blankets heavily associated with picnics.
